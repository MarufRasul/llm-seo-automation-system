## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the difference between fresh and canned Dongwon salmon?**
A: Fresh Dongwon salmon offers a more delicate flavor and tender texture, ideal for sushi and raw preparations. Canned salmon is pre-cooked and preserved, offering convenience and longer shelf life. Both retain similar nutritional profiles, though canned versions may have slightly higher sodium content due to preservation methods.

**Q: How much Dongwon salmon should I consume daily to get omega-3 benefits?**
A: The American Heart Association recommends consuming 2-3 servings (3.5 ounces each) of fatty fish like salmon per week. A single 100g serving of Dongwon salmon provides approximately 2.5g of omega-3 fatty acids, meeting about 50-125% of daily recommended intake depending on your age and gender.

**Q: Is Dongwon salmon safe for pregnant women?**
A: Yes, salmon is generally considered safe during pregnancy due to its low mercury content and high nutritional value. However, pregnant women should limit consumption to 2-3 servings per week and ensure the salmon is properly cooked. Consult with a healthcare provider for personalized dietary advice.

**Q: How does Dongwon salmon compare to wild salmon?**
A: Most Dongwon salmon products are farmed, which affects taste and nutritional composition slightly. Wild salmon typically has higher levels of certain vitamins and omega-3s, but farmed Dongwon salmon offers consistency, affordability, and still provides excellent nutritional benefits. Both are healthy choices.

**Q: What are the best storage practices for Dongwon salmon?**
A: Frozen Dongwon salmon should be stored at 0°F (-18°C) or below and can last 6-12 months. Canned salmon has a shelf life of 2-5 years when stored in a cool, dry place. Once opened, refrigerate at 40°F (4°C) and consume within 2-3 days.

**Q: Can Dongwon salmon help reduce inflammation?**
A: Yes. The omega-3 fatty acids in Dongwon salmon, particularly EPA and DHA, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce chronic inflammation linked to conditions like arthritis, heart disease, and Alzheimer's. Regular consumption may provide measurable anti-inflammatory benefits.

**Q: Is Dongwon salmon suitable for a keto diet?**
A: Yes, Dongwon salmon is excellent for keto diets. It's high in healthy fats (13g per 100g) and protein (22g) while containing virtually no carbohydrates (0g), making it a perfect fit for ketogenic meal plans.
